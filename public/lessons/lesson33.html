üîê What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting computers, networks, and data from digital attacks, unauthorized access, and damage. It is crucial for maintaining the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information.

üí• Types of Cyber Threats

  • Malware: Malicious software like viruses, worms, and trojans.
  • Phishing: Fraudulent attempts to steal sensitive data via fake emails or websites.
  • DDoS Attacks: Overloading a system or server with traffic to bring it down.
  • Ransomware: Locking data until a ransom is paid.
  • Insider Threats: Security breaches caused by employees or trusted individuals.

üõ°Ô∏è Key Concepts

  • Encryption: Converting data into a coded format for protection.
  • Firewalls: Software or hardware that blocks unauthorized access.
  • Antivirus: Tools that detect and remove malware.
  • Authentication: Verifying user identity using passwords, biometrics, etc.
  • Backup: Creating copies of data to restore in case of loss or attack.

  • Companies (to secure customer info & systems)
  • Governments (to protect critical infrastructure)
  • Schools, hospitals, banks, and more
