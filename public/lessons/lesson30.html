üìå What is SDLC?

The Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) is a process for planning, creating, testing, and deploying software. It ensures software is delivered efficiently, on time, and meets quality standards.

üìà Phases of SDLC

  1. Requirement Gathering: Understanding what the client or market needs.
  2. System Design: Planning the system architecture and software components.
  3. Implementation (Coding): Developers write the actual program code.
  4. Testing: Verifying the software is working as expected (unit, integration, system tests).
  5. Deployment: Delivering the system to users or production environment.
  6. Maintenance: Fixing bugs and updating software after deployment.

‚öôÔ∏è SDLC Models

  • Waterfall: Linear, step-by-step process. Easy but rigid.
  • Agile: Iterative and flexible, includes sprints and regular feedback.
  • Spiral: Combines design and prototyping with risk analysis.
  • V-Model: Similar to waterfall, but emphasizes verification and validation.

üì¶ Why SDLC is Important

  • Helps manage complex software projects
  • Reduces errors and costs
  • Improves product quality and customer satisfaction
  • Provides clear structure and accountability
