üîí What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting systems, networks, and data from digital attacks. It includes defending against unauthorized access, data breaches, malware, and other cyber threats.

üí£ Common Cyber Threats

  • Malware: Software designed to harm a system (viruses, worms, trojans).
  • Phishing: Fraudulent attempts to obtain sensitive info via fake emails or websites.
  • Ransomware: Malware that locks data until a ransom is paid.
  • Denial of Service (DoS): Attacks that overwhelm systems and cause them to crash.
  • Man-in-the-Middle (MitM): Attacker intercepts communication between two parties.

üîê Cybersecurity Practices

  • Use Strong Passwords: Combine upper/lowercase, numbers, and symbols.
  • Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): Add an extra layer beyond just passwords.
  • Keep Software Updated: Regular updates fix known vulnerabilities.
  • Use Firewalls and Antivirus: Helps detect and block threats.
  • Back Up Data: Frequently back up files to avoid total loss.
