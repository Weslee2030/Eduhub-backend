üß≠ What is Ethics in Computing?

Ethics in computing refers to the moral principles and guidelines that govern how technology is developed and used. It ensures that computing professionals act with honesty, fairness, and respect for others' rights and dignity.

üåç Why is Ethics Important?

  • Prevents harm to users and society
  • Protects privacy and sensitive data
  • Encourages fairness and reduces bias
  • Promotes accountability for tech decisions

üîë Key Ethical Issues

  • Data Privacy: Respecting user confidentiality and secure handling of personal data.
  • Digital Rights: Ensuring fair access and control over digital content and tools.
  • Bias & Discrimination: Avoiding unfair treatment through biased algorithms or decisions.
  • Intellectual Property: Respecting creators' rights over software, images, code, and ideas.
  • Cyberbullying & Harassment: Combating online abuse and harmful behavior.
