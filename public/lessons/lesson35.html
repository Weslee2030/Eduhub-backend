The Internet of Things (IoT) refers to a network of physical devices‚Äîsuch as home appliances, vehicles, wearables, and sensors‚Äîembedded with electronics, software, and connectivity that allow them to collect and exchange data.

üì° Key Components

  • Devices/Sensors: Collect data from the environment (e.g., temperature, motion).
  • Connectivity: Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, cellular, or satellite connections to send data.
  • Cloud Computing: Stores and processes data from IoT devices.
  • User Interface: Apps or dashboards used to monitor and control devices.

üè† Common IoT Applications

  • Smart Homes: Thermostats, lights, and locks controlled via apps.
  • Healthcare: Wearables like smartwatches for heart rate monitoring.
  • Industry: Smart factories with predictive maintenance systems.
  • Agriculture: Soil and crop monitoring for smart irrigation.
  • Transportation: Vehicle tracking, smart parking, autonomous cars.
