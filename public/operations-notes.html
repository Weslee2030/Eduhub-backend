1. Introduction to Operations Management

Definition: Operations management involves designing, overseeing, and controlling the processes that transform inputs into outputs to meet customer needs.

Purpose: Ensures efficiency, quality, and sustainability while meeting business objectives.

  • Minimize costs while maintaining quality
  • Maximize efficiency and productivity
  • Meet customer expectations

Connection to CUEGIS: Links to change, culture, ethics, globalization, innovation, and strategy.

2. Production Methods

Job Production: Custom-made, one-off items (e.g., wedding dresses)

Batch Production: Limited identical products in groups (e.g., bakery batches)

Mass Production: High-volume, standardized items (e.g., smartphones)

Mass Customization: Customizable products at scale (e.g., Nike custom shoes)

Cellular Manufacturing (HL): Production organized into teams or cells

3. Quality Management (HL)

  • Quality Circles: Employee groups improving quality
  • Benchmarking: Comparing with industry leaders
  • Total Quality Management (TQM): Focus on quality in every process
  • Lean Production: Eliminating waste (JIT, Kaizen, Kanban)
  • International Standards: ISO 9001 ensures consistency

4. Location of Production

  • Proximity to Market
  • Access to Raw Materials
  • Labor Availability & Costs
  • Infrastructure
  • Government Incentives
  • Ethical/Environmental Considerations

Ways to Reorganize Production: Outsourcing, Offshoring, Insourcing

5. Stock Control (HL)

  • Key Terms: Lead time, Buffer stock, Reorder level, Reorder quantity
  • Stock Control Chart: Visual inventory management tool
  • JIT vs JIC: Minimal stock vs High buffer stock strategies

6. Crisis Management & Contingency Planning (HL)

Crisis Management: Reactive response to unexpected disruptions

Contingency Planning: Proactive preparation for potential crises

  • Key Factors: Transparency, Communication, Speed, Control
  • Pros: Faster recovery, better safety, protects brand
  • Cons: Costly, time-consuming, may become outdated

7. Sustainability and Operations

Sustainable Production: Eco-friendly practices to reduce environmental impact

Example: IKEA uses renewable materials and efficient logistics

8. Analytical Tools

  • SWOT Analysis: Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats
  • Porter‚Äôs Five Forces: Industry competitiveness analysis
  • Value Chain: Adds value and reduces waste in operations

9. Case Studies

  • Toyota: Lean production & TQM; affected by 2011 earthquake
  • Apple: Outsourcing to Foxconn; ethical issues
  • IKEA: Sustainability focus and cost efficiency

10. Exam Tips

  • Use real-world examples
  • Apply CUEGIS concepts
  • Structure answers for AO3 with evaluation
  • Be ready to draw charts (e.g., stock control)
